Multimedia Graphic User Interface "GUI" on the monitor behind the aircraft seat

  • Client : IranAir / Airbus
  • Date : 2017
  • Skills : Illustrator, Photoshop

One of my great and challenging experiences was designing the multimedia monitors behind the aircraft seats, when new aircraft entered the Iran Air "HOMA" fleet. After receiving information from GEE and Airbus companies, for reducing costs in the critical conditions of Homa Airlines, we proposed a redesign of an existing GUI without any code change. At first, the company was worried about the UI, via our proposed approach we reduced the problems in design without changing the code, and the costs were reduced almost 75% with a little gimmick! ;)

I tried to notice that the GUI for international airlines would be specific for each country mixed with modern design. For IranAir GUI, I prefered to use elements showing the Iranian culture because IranAir Logo (Homa Ancient Bird) is derived from one of the most well-known architectural designs at Persepolis with great graphics capabilities in different designs, so I designed a traditional motif from the analyzed design of Sepahsalar Mosque and I reached out to a unique motif finally. In the meantime, I finished the design of Iran Air's Guideline (it can be seen in the: Portfolio -> Unique -> Guideline).
